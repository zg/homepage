Amateur Radio

I have recently started to venture into learning more about amateur radio (also known as ham radio). I have always been fascinated by radio technology, but haven't had the time to learn how it works. Now that I have graduated from university, I have more time on my hands and in late September 2016, I started learning about amateur radio.

My girlfriend, Denisse, is a Computer Engineering student at RIT and I figured that it would be a good motivation for both of us to try and learn enough about radio technology so that the two of us could communicate with one another while she is still attending university in New York. So, as of writing this, my goal is to accomplish transcontinental radio communication.

My plans as of now are to obtain my Technician and General license through ARRL and then proceed to build my own pieces of radio equipment before purchasing professional equipment. I have read numerous times in the ARRL Ham Radio License Manual that taking this approach is a valuable experience and it will help you understand the internals of the radio technology you use. This helps one to debug certain issues that may arise when working with the equipment that they use.
